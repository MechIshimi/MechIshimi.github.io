About

Mechanical engineer with a background in structural fabrication, hardware test and validation, and rapid prototyping. Drawn to high-ownership environments where design decisions have direct hardware consequences and iteration is measured in days, not quarters.

At CMU Racing I designed and fabricated chassis components, led TIG welding operations across steel and titanium, and built a torsional stiffness validation rig from scratch. At Westinghouse I owned test procedures and hardware validation on the eVinci microreactor program in a fast-paced, small-team environment.

Currently pursuing an FAA Private Pilot License and Part 107 commercial UAS certificate. Seeking roles in defense and aerospace where the engineering is close to the metal.

Mechanical Energy Recovery Bicycle
CMU Capstone · Most Innovative — Design Expo

Designed and manufactured a purely mechanical drivetrain that stores kinetic energy via an elastic mechanism during deceleration and releases it at low speeds. No electronics, no batteries. Operates entirely through mechanical advantage and material elasticity, requiring precise tolerance control and iterative fabrication.

Mechanical Design CAD Fabrication Drivetrain Award Winner
